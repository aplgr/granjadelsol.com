    Location

    José Fassardi is a small town with about 5,100 inhabitants (as of 2022) and is located in the Guairá administrative district.


    The property is situated north of the town center in a quiet and rural environment, offering plenty of space for relaxation and nature. It is accessible via a side road that is passable in all weather conditions. In the immediate vicinity, there are small shops (despensas) for daily needs.


    The center of José Fassardi is only about 2 km away. There you will find various shopping options, medical facilities, as well as elementary and secondary schools. Additionally, there is a central bus stop from which buses depart daily to various destinations, such as Villarrica.


    Alternatively, you can use the newly paved road just 500 meters from the property to reach Planta Urbana (Independencia) by car in about 40 minutes. The cities of Villarrica and Caazapá are also easily accessible by car in about an hour via well-developed, paved roads. There you have access to additional shopping opportunities, cooperatives, and cultural and medical facilities.


    Whether you are looking for the advantage of proximity to the city or prefer rural tranquility – the location of the property offers you a balanced mix of both. The infrastructure and connections make it possible to live in nature while still having quick access to urban conveniences.

    What exactly is the "title" about?
    They are an official document that shows who the legal owner of the land is. They are needed to sell or transfer the property. Without them, no legal sale is possible.
    How does the buying process work?
    The buying process in Paraguay has a few steps:
    1. First meeting with the notary:
      • Sign a preliminary contract.
      • Pay a deposit.
      • The notary checks the ownership papers (takes about 2–4 weeks).

    2. Second meeting with the notary:
      • Sign the purchase contract.
      • Pay the rest of the purchase price.
      • The notary starts the transfer of ownership (takes about 4–8 weeks).

    After full payment, the property belongs to the new owner, who can use it immediately. The issuance of the new title is a mere formality. Once completed, the Escribana will contact the new owner to collect the document.

    What costs come with buying the land?

    In addition to the purchase price, there are other so-called ancillary costs that are borne by the buyer.
    These include, in particular, the costs for the legal transfer of the property (“transfer costs”) and, if involved, the real estate agent fees.

    Transfer Costs

    These are the official costs for transferring the land to the new owner. They are paid at the notary’s office and usually range between about 1.5% and 4.5% of the purchase price. The notary will calculate them in advance and explain clearly.

    They include for example:

    • Notary fees: Preparing and confirming the purchase contract and handling the legal transfer.
    • Land registry fees: Registering the new owner in the national land registry.
    • Taxes: Depending on location and purchase price, some state or local taxes may apply.
    • Administrative fees: For example at cadastral offices or tax offices.
    Real estate agent Fees

    Currently, no agent is involved, so no real estate agent fees are incurred. Should an agent be commissioned during the course of the transaction — regardless of whether by the buyer or seller — the buyer shall bear the corresponding fees.

    Do contracts have to be in Spanish?
    Yes, in Paraguay contracts must be written in Spanish and understood by both sides to be valid. If you don’t speak Spanish well, a certified translator should be present. The costs depend on travel and the length of the meeting.
