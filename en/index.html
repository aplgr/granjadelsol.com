    Welcome!

    Imagine sitting on your terrace and experiencing three extraordinary moments: the sunrise bathing the pastures and the Ybytyruzu mountain range in soft light, the Paraguayan sunset with its shades of orange and red, or a breathtaking view of the mountain range with Paraguay's highest peak, Cerro Tres Kandu.

    On our 20-hectare property, Granja del Sol, you can enjoy these moments every day. With over 300 sunny days a year and plenty of space, you can bring your own vision of life in nature to reality—whether as a family, investor, or self-sufficient individual.

    For families, this property offers ample space to live together in nature—be it for children, grandparents, animals, or your own garden. It is a place to experience the diverse possibilities of nature and pursue your own projects.

    For investors, this property holds interesting potential for future sustainable projects that offer both ecological and economic benefits. Whether in forestry, small agricultural ventures, or for agro-tourism and recreation, the property provides various opportunities for use.

    For self-sufficient individuals, there is plenty of room to lead a nature-oriented lifestyle, grow your own vegetables, keep animals, and enjoy the independence of rural living. Various water sources and the forest offer additional support for these endeavors.

    Whatever your vision may be, Granja del Sol offers the space and tranquility to realize your projects and live in a green, peaceful environment.

    Location

    José Fassardi is a small town with approximately 5,100 inhabitants (as of 2022) and is located in the Guairá district.

    The property is situated north of the town center in a quiet, rural setting, offering ample space for relaxation and nature. It is accessible via a side road that is navigable in all weather conditions. The town center of José Fassardi is just about 2 km away, where you can find supermarkets, hardware stores, doctors, and a central bus station. In the immediate vicinity, there are small local shops ("Despensen") for daily needs.

    The location of the property combines the tranquility of rural life with convenient regional access. Within just 500 meters, you can reach a newly asphalted road that connects José Fassardi with Planta Urbana (Independencia). There, you will find additional essential shops, including the well-known supermarket "Almacen 50" and the Pfannl Cooperative. The cities of Villarrica and Caazapá can also be reached in about an hour via well-maintained, asphalted roads.

    Whether you seek the convenience of proximity to the city or the peace of the countryside, the property's location offers a balanced mix of both. The infrastructure and connectivity make it possible to live in nature while easily accessing urban amenities.

    What ongoing costs should I expect?

    An annual property tax is required. In 2024, this was 284,000 PYG (approximately €35). This amount depends on government regulations and can change annually. Electricity is provided by ANDE, which charges 310 PYG (approximately €0.04) per kWh. No other ongoing costs apply. (As of 12/2024)

    Can the property be divided into smaller plots?

    Yes, the property can be divided into smaller plots without issue. Such a division is generally possible and offers various usage options.

    What is the soil quality of the property?

    The property has clay-rich, fertile soil, which is excellent for agricultural use. The good water retention ensures stable moisture levels, especially during the warm summer months. Compared to other regions in Paraguay where sandy soils lose water quickly, this property offers a particularly advantageous foundation for efficient irrigation. Additionally, the vegetable patch has already been enriched with manure, further improving soil quality.

    Are there neighbors, or is the property remote?

    The property is located in a rural area. The two adjacent neighboring properties are uninhabited. Paraguayan neighbors live approximately 200 meters away. In the area around José Fassardi, several German families have also settled.

    Does the property have a title?

    Yes, the property title is available. The transfer of the property is therefore possible immediately.

    What exactly is the "title" about?

    The title is an official document that identifies the rightful owner of the property. It is necessary to sell or transfer the property. Without this title, no legally valid sale can take place.

    How does the purchase process work?

    The purchase process in Paraguay involves several steps:

    1. First appointment with the Escribana (Notary):
      • Signing of a preliminary contract.
      • Payment of a deposit.
      • Formal verification of the title by the Escribana (Duration: 2–4 weeks).
    2. Second appointment with the Escribana:
      • Signing of the purchase contract.
      • Payment of the remaining purchase price.
      • The Escribana initiates the ownership transfer (Duration: 4–8 weeks).

    After full payment, the property belongs to the new owner, who can use it immediately. Issuing the new title is a mere formality. Once completed, the Escribana will notify the new owner to collect the document.

    What fees are involved?

    The fees for the Escribana are legally regulated and depend on the purchase price. Typically, one should expect about 5% of the purchase amount. In our case, the cost was 4.5%.

    Do purchase contracts have to be concluded in Spanish?

    Yes, in Paraguay, purchase contracts must be drafted in Spanish and understood by both parties to be legally valid. If Spanish is not confidently spoken, a certified translator should be hired. The cost depends on travel and the duration of the appointment.
